<div class="eyebrow">The Broken Pipeline</div>

## Precise but Fragile

Many organizations believe they are “data-driven” because they have dashboards, KPIs, and machine learning models. But the actual decision pipeline often looks like this:

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   **The Dashboard Trap**
    ---
    `Data → Dashboard → Gut Feeling → Decision`
    
    Organizations become “data-driven,” but dashboards mainly serve to train gut feeling.

-   **The Proxy Trap**
    ---
    `Data → ML Prediction → KPI Optimization → Decision`
    
    Machine learning generates point predictions optimized for proxy metrics, blinding the organization to uncertainty.

</div>

The result is a pipeline that works until the world changes. **And decisions change the world.**

<div class="eyebrow">Core Philosophy</div>

## Fitting data is not understanding the world.

A model can fit historical data extremely well and still fail catastrophically when decisions change the environment.

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   **The Flaw of Averages**
    ---
    Decision-makers often optimize for the “most likely” scenario while ignoring nonlinear effects. But averages systematically break nonlinear systems: $E[f(x)] \neq f(E[x])$.

-   **Causal Salads**
    ---
    Organizations often optimize metrics whose relationship to real utility is poorly understood. The result: disconnected KPIs and fragile decisions.

-   **Distribution Shift**
    ---
    A prediction trained on historical data cannot reliably evaluate an intervention that changes the data-generating process. 

-   **Distributions, Not Points**
    ---
    A single predicted number hides uncertainty. Decision-makers need distributions, scenario analysis, and action-conditional forecasts.

</div>
